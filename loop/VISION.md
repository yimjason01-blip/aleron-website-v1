# Aleron MD Vision (verifier north star)

Most longevity brands sell not dying. The result is sterile (biomarkers, scans, dashboards) or vain (looking 35 forever). Both are repulsive futures. Aleron sells a different belief: **you might be just getting started.**

The attractor state is a person with full cognitive clarity and full physical capability decades from now, mid-experience, visibly alive. If you truly believe you have multiple fully-capable decades ahead, you plan differently now. Prevention stops being a tax on the present and becomes the cost basis of a longer arc you are excited to live.

The feeling is **future nostalgia**: missing, in advance, a future you now intend to have. The proof stack: the capability curve (your trajectory is being written now, and it bends), the physician (one person who sees the whole picture), and the engine (genome, blood, wearables, history read together).

One line: **Aleron MD exists to make "I'm just getting started" a medically defensible position at any age.**

Buyer test: an executive looks at this and thinks "that's me in 10 years, and I'm behind on getting there." Worth far more than $4,200/yr, which is why it sells at $4,200/yr.

## Verifier tests (apply to every section)

1. **Future nostalgia test** — does the section make the reader miss a future they haven't had yet?
2. **Disciplined vitality test** — does every element earn its place? No decoration, no element without a job.
3. **Confidence-not-vanity test** — would this embarrass a surgeon? Show capability, not aesthetics. Say the specific true thing, never adjectives.
4. **Tribe/singular test** — imagery shows experiences (solo runner, surfers together), never patients, models, or clinics.
5. **Magnetism test** — hero subjects read 45-60, visibly fitter than the viewer at "5 honest years of work" distance, not "different species" distance. Reject both failure modes: weathered survivor AND airbrushed immortal. The 70s appear in narrative and the curve only, never cast in imagery.
