# Aleron MD Landing Page: Messaging Rack (MESSAGING)

The complete top-to-bottom copy spine for the Aleron MD landing page. Every headline and
paragraph below is final copy, ready to set. Builders implement in this order.

**Audience:** prospective members (patients) first, employers/brokers second.
**Voice:** physician-led, honest, casual-premium. Specific true things, not adjectives.
**Brand lane:** lowercase-a navy / bronze / rust / cream. Square edges. Restrained, scroll-driven motion.

**Binding copy conventions used throughout:**
- No em dashes anywhere. Periods and commas only.
- Header CTA is `Log In`. Acquisition CTA is `Apply for Membership`. Never `Join`.
- Clinical values always carry units (e.g. "Lp(a)", "ApoB", "163-gene panel").
- Brand reads `Aleron MD` on full reference, `Aleron` in running prose, matching the deck.

## Section 1: Hero

**Job:** Land the emotional promise in one breath, before any explanation. Make a high-income,
time-poor person feel that the good years ahead are real and worth protecting. Establish the
premium, human, physician-led tone. Get the right person to keep scrolling.

**Headline (PRIMARY):**
> Your best days are still ahead.

**Headline (ALT):**
> Most of your best years are still unwritten.

(Primary keeps the current anchor and its spirit. Alt trades the reassurance for agency, and
sets up the curve section's "still unwritten" idea more directly. Recommend primary for launch.)

**Supporting copy (final):**
> Physician-led preventive medicine, powered by AI and your own genetic, lab, and wearable data.
> One physician who sees the whole picture, and a plan for what matters next.

**Proof elements:**
- The two-person layered hero imagery (runner, surfer): active, mid-life, healthy people, not a
  clinic stock photo. The image is the proof of the promise.
- `aleron MD` wordmark, top left. `Log In` top right.

**Mechanism note:**
Layered, parallax hero carousel from the deck (background plane, geometric block plane, subject
plane, anchored color block). Two slides (runner, then surfer) cross-fade with horizontal
parallax on scroll and on dot click. This is the first signal that the site behaves like the
pitch-deck demo, not a brochure. Square navy and rust blocks frame the copy, no rounded corners.

**CTA:**
- Primary: `Apply for Membership`
- Header: `Log In`

## Section 2: The Capability Curve (the teaching moment)

**Job:** The central idea of the whole page. Teach the visitor that "health" is really their
body's capacity to do what they want, that this capacity follows a curve over their life, that
the slope is being set right now by what they do and don't catch, and that the curve is not
fixed. This is where a skeptic becomes a believer. It must feel earned, not asserted.

This section advances through four scroll-driven stages. The headline and the curve redraw at
each stage. Stage labels (Intro / Capability / Hazards / Future) sit on a right-hand rail and
double as scroll progress.

### Stage 1: Intro

**Headline (PRIMARY):**
> The story of your future is written on top of this curve.

**Headline (ALT):**
> This curve is the rest of your life. Most of it is still unwritten.

(Primary is the figma/deck anchor and reads more calmly. Alt is more provocative and ties back to
the hero alt. Recommend primary.)

**Supporting copy (final):**
> One line, from age 30 to 90. Watch what it does.

### Stage 2: Capability

**Headline:**
> It's your body's ability to do what you want in life.

**Supporting copy (final):**
> High on the curve, you do anything you want. Lower down, you start depending on other people.
> The same line decides whether your seventies look like hard hikes or hard stairs.

**Proof elements (the functional bands rendered on the Y axis):**
- Endurance athlete
- Hard recreation
- Stairs without thinking
- Independent daily life
- Strained effort
- Needs help

(These map VO2max to lived capability. Zone labels on the chart: **Do Anything You Want** above,
**Dependent On Others** below.)

### Stage 3: Hazards

**Headline:**
> The slope is being written right now, quietly, by what you do and don't prevent.

**Supporting copy (final):**
> A serious event does not just cost you a bad year. It drops the whole curve and it rarely comes
> all the way back. Most of these are visible years before they happen, in markers a standard
> physical never runs.

**Proof element:** a `Serious Life Event` marker on the curve that drops the line sharply below
its prior path, with a dashed ghost line showing where the curve would have gone.

### Stage 4: Future

**Headline:**
> The curve is not destiny. Aleron can help you plot a new trajectory.

**Supporting copy (final):**
> Catch the risk early, train the right things, and the line lifts and stretches. The work is not
> adding empty years. It is keeping you in the part of the curve where life is still yours to run.

**Proof element:** a `Stronger Golden Years` marker. The lifted curve sits above the dashed
default curve and holds high capability years longer.

**Mechanism note:**
This is the live VO2max-vs-age teaching tool from the deck, transplanted whole. Scroll drives the
four stages; the curve and color ramp (rust at high capability, fading to navy at the floor)
redraw between stages. Below the chart, three sliders, **Conditioning**, **Lifestyle**, and
**Underlying Risk**, let the visitor move their own curve in real time and see the bands they keep
or lose. This is the single most important interactive element on the page. It must feel like the
deck demo: a thing you play with, not a picture you look at.

**CTA:** none here. The interaction is the payload. (Optional quiet inline link: `See how Aleron reads your curve` jumping to Section 4.)

## Section 4: What Aleron MD Actually Does (the engine, step by step)

**Job:** Make the product concrete and specific. Walk through onboarding to a physician-led plan
so the visitor can picture themselves doing it. This is where vague "longevity care" becomes a
real sequence with real partners, panels, and turnaround times. Defeat the "this could be any
concierge clinic" reflex with specifics.

**Section headline (final):**
> Getting your Aleron MD plan is straightforward.

**Section supporting copy (final):**
> No forty-page intake. No waiting room. Four steps from sign-up to a plan a physician stands behind.

The four steps run as a carousel. Each step is its own headline, copy, and proof.

### Step 01: Onboarding

**Headline:**
> Onboarding takes fifteen minutes.

**Copy (final):**
> Demographics, family history, lifestyle, and screening history, collected the way every other
> app on your phone does it. Then your wearable connects in two taps and starts streaming the
> signals your physician will actually use.

**Proof:** "Connects with" logo wall: Apple Watch, Polar, Whoop, Garmin, Fitbit, Eight Sleep,
Dexcom, Withings.

### Step 02: Genetics

**Headline:**
> A small kit arrives at your door.

**Copy (final):**
> Spit in the tube, drop it in the prepaid mailer. The 163-gene Invitae panel screens for the
> variants that actually change a care plan: moderate-penetrance cancer risks, cardiovascular
> variants, and pharmacogenomic markers that change which drugs work for you.

**Proof:** "Genetic testing by Invitae." Stats: **163 genes** (Invitae panel), **~3 weeks** (results return).

### Step 03: Bloodwork

**Headline:**
> Bloodwork on your terms.

**Copy (final):**
> Draw the same Elite panel two ways. Walk into your nearest Quest patient service center, or skip
> the trip with an at-home capillary kit that ships to your door. Either way you get a full
> metabolic, cardiovascular, and inflammatory workup, including the ApoB and Lp(a) markers most
> physicals never order.

**Proof:** "Labs through Quest." The Elite panel includes, shown as marker groups:
- Core panel: CBC with differential, CMP, liver markers, kidney markers
- Cardiometabolic: lipid panel, ApoB, Lp(a), HbA1c, fasting insulin, uric acid
- Inflammation: hsCRP, homocysteine, ferritin, iron/TIBC
- Thyroid and nutrients: TSH, free T4, vitamin D, B12, folate
- Depth: UACR and 80+ additional markers across metabolic, cardiovascular, inflammatory, renal,
  hepatic, and micronutrient domains

### Step 04: Your plan

**Headline:**
> A physician in your corner, on your schedule.

**Copy (final):**
> Aleron combines labs, genetics, wearable trends, records, and physician judgment into a
> risk picture, domain by domain. Talk it through live on a video visit, or handle it
> asynchronously and message your care team whenever it suits you. The output is not a report.
> It is a plan for what matters next.

**Proof:** Stats: **5 domains** (risk models run), **1** (physician-led game plan).
Split visual: "Live visit" / "Async."

**Mechanism note:**
Tabbed carousel from the deck experience section: a left copy pane and a right image pane that
swap together. Auto-advances every five seconds only while in view, pauses on hover, and a step
rail plus progress bar lets the visitor drive. Steps 03 and 04 use split images (in-person vs at
home, live vs async) to show the choice without extra words.

**CTA:** quiet inline `Apply for Membership` at the end of the carousel.

## Section 5: It Keeps Working (continuous care)

**Job:** Kill the "one expensive checkup" objection. Show that membership is an ongoing
relationship, not a single scan-and-report. This is where the recurring value, and the price,
gets justified.

**Headline (final):**
> This is just the start.

**Supporting copy (final):**
> Aleron doesn't hand you a report and disappear. It works for you between visits, every day.

**Proof elements (six modules):**
1. **Aleron AI, grounded in your data.** It stays on between visits, grounded in your labs,
   genetics, wearables, imaging, and care plan. It watches for changes and keeps your physician current.
2. **Continuous biometric monitoring.** Sleep, HRV, resting heart rate, and activity, tracked and
   analyzed over time. We catch the signal before you feel the symptom.
3. **Twice-yearly plan review.** Aleron reviews labs, wearable trends, records, symptoms, goals,
   and new research, then updates your priorities around what actually changed in your risk.
4. **Nutrition coaching, built into your plan.** Guidance shaped by your labs, goals, preferences,
   and metabolic risk, not a generic meal plan.
5. **Behavioral therapist support.** Help turning priorities into routines and follow-through that
   fit a real life.
6. **Unlimited physician access.** 24/7 telemedicine with a physician who already has your full
   picture: your data, your genetics, your history. No repeating yourself.

**Mechanism note:** six-card grid on navy, with the dark feature icons from the deck. Cards rise
in on scroll in a short stagger. Restrained, no hover bounce.

**CTA:** none (sets up the human moment next).

**Headline:** none. The quote is the headline.

**Attribution:** J.L., member since 2025.

not a five-star wall.

**Mechanism note:** full-bleed navy-to-rust gradient panel, large quote set in the display face.
The gradient is the one warm, atmospheric moment on the page. Quote fades up on scroll. No avatar
stock photo, the words carry it.

**CTA:** none.

## Section 7: Proof (what the engine actually finds)

**Job:** Convert the believer with evidence. Show that this is not luxury theater, that running
the full picture changes real medical decisions for real people. Every number here is sourced
from published literature applied to an adult preventive population, not invented.

**Headline (final):**
> Most members leave their first review with a care plan their PCP wouldn't have given them.

**Supporting copy (final):**
> Same labs. Same body. Different level of care.

**Proof elements (stat rack):**

| Figure | What it means |
|---|---|
| **63%** | of new members get a plan that materially differs from what their PCP would do. |
| **12%** | receive an actionable genetic finding: a pathogenic variant that changes screening or medication for life, almost never tested in standard care. |
| **4 in 10** | receive a pharmacogenomic flag: a drug picked or dosed differently, from statin metabolism to SSRI response to clopidogrel non-response. |
| **35%** | show a CAC reclassification: coronary calcium changes the statin decision in roughly one in three intermediate-risk patients. |
| **1 in 5** | find Lp(a) elevated: a lifetime cardiovascular-risk reclassification plus family screening, from a marker rarely measured in standard panels. |

**Required source line (set as fine print under the rack, verbatim):**
> Drivers overlap; the headline figure is the union, not the sum. Drawn from PREVENT (AHA 2023),
> CAC reclassification studies (MESA, CAC Consortium), and pharmacogenomics plus clinical-grade
> genetic-panel actionable yield in adult preventive populations.

**Optional supporting proof (from member interviews, deck):**
> Of the people we built this for, 96% believe the right medical team could bend their curve. Only
> 9% believe they can find that team. The money was never the barrier. Clarity and time were.

(Use the interview stat only if a second proof beat is wanted. It is real: 23 interviews, 16
executives, 4 physicians, 3 VCs.)

**Mechanism note:** left-aligned headline, right-hand stat column where each figure is large and
quiet, counting up once on scroll into view. Tabular figures. No charts here, the numbers are the
visual.

**CTA:** none (flows into the close).
